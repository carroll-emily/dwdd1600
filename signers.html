Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (adopted July 4, 1776) include:

John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress, remembered for his bold, oversized signature at the top.
Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Respected statesman, inventor, and writer; key diplomat in securing French support.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Forceful advocate for independence who later became the second U.S. president.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Prominent leader of colonial resistance and organizer of revolutionary committees.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign the Continental Association, the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer and the last surviving signer (died 1832).

The signers effect lies in their collective commitment to independence, which legitimized the Declaration, unified the colonies, and set the foundation for the United States as a sovereign nation.
